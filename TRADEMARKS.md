## Allowed Without Asking

You may, without prior permission:

- refer to Grimoire truthfully in commentary, documentation, blog posts, reviews, comparisons, or tutorials
- say that your project is based on, compatible with, or derived from Grimoire, as long as that statement is accurate
- redistribute unmodified official releases while preserving existing notices
- make nominative use of the Grimoire name to explain that you are discussing the original project
- use screenshots of unmodified Grimoire for reviews, tutorials, issue reports, or documentation

## Not Allowed Without Written Permission

You may not, without prior written permission:

- call a modified product, fork, hosted service, or commercial offering "Grimoire"
- use the Grimoire name or logo in a way that suggests your version is official
- use the Grimoire logo, app icon, or wordmark in product branding, app-store listings, domains, social profiles, marketing pages, or paid services for a fork
- imply endorsement, affiliation, sponsorship, or approval by the Grimoire project or Srinivas Pendela when none exists
- register confusingly similar domains, package names, app names, or organization names for derivative products

## Forks And Modified Versions

You are welcome to fork or modify Grimoire under the AGPL. Redistributed, hosted, or packaged modified versions must use their own visible product name and branding unless you have written permission to use the Grimoire marks.

Good examples:

- "Fork of Grimoire"
- "Based on Grimoire"
- "Internal knowledge app derived from Grimoire"

Bad examples:

- calling a modified product "Grimoire"
- using the Grimoire logo for a fork
- presenting a hosted fork as the official Grimoire product
